About Me

I am currently a third-year undergraduate student. I am interested in graph neural network driven anomaly detection, and a related work is expected to be produced in the near future. Although neural networks are powerful tools, I still hope to explore them more deeply and improve their interpretability. This idea is also reflected in my recent work: knowing what happens is important, but understanding why it happens matters even more :). If the paper is accepted, I will release the code publicly.

I am also interested in 3D Unity driven human-computer interaction. The project repositories below are developed from my personal interests. Since I like G.E.M. and Charles Leclerc, I built a digital character that can dance to music and a racing game where players can compete with AI racing cars. Pull requests and suggestions are welcome.

Recently, I have also been paying attention to AI agents. While being impressed by the engineering ability of advanced agent assistants, I developed a multi-agent AI learning assistant with two teammates for the China Software Cup competition. Suggestions and pull requests are also welcome.
